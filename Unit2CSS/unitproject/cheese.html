Get to Know Cheese

What is Cheese?

"Cheese is a dairy product produced in a range of flavors, textures, and forms by coagulation of the milk protein casein. It comprises proteins and fat from milk (usually the milk of cows, buffalo, goats or sheep)."(Wikipedia)

Fun Facts About Cheese

  • The production of cheese predates recorded history, beginning possibily over 7000 years ago!
  • Humans most likely created cheese by accident by using sacks made out of animal stomachs, to carry milk, that caused curdling.
  • Cows that have a grassy diet, rich in beta-carotene,cause the cheese to be tinted orange.
  • There is Donkey and Reindeer Cheese even!
  • Casu martzu is a Sardinian sheep milk cheese that contains live maggots and is deadly to eat if the maggots are dead.
  • In Denmark, the average person eats 62 lbs per year. In Finland, its 61 lbs and in Iceland its 60 lbs.
  • The Chinese eat the least amount of cheese!
  • A Serbian donkey cheese, Pule, can sell for over $600 per pound!
